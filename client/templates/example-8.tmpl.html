Notice that even though this directive is nested within an ng-view whose parent is MainCtrl, We don't have access to MainCtrl's scope because this directive has declared an isolate scope.

By transcluding a directive (seNgViewChild) which doesn't have an isolate scope and using require seNgView, we have bypassed the isolate scope on seNgView and injected a parent scope into seNgView. Don't actually do this, it's just to illustrate an example!
